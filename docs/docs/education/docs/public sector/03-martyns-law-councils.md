# Martyn's Law Compliance for Local Authorities

**Legislation:** Terrorism (Protection of Premises) Act 2025
**Enforcement date:** April 2027
**Source:** Fyrfly Systems Ltd — [www.fyrflysystems.com/connected-council.html](https://www.fyrflysystems.com/connected-council.html)

---

## Scope for Local Authorities

Local authorities almost certainly operate multiple venues in both tiers:

**Standard Tier (100–799 capacity):** Libraries, council offices, civic meeting rooms, managed car parks, smaller community halls.

**Enhanced Tier (800+ capacity):** Civic halls, leisure centres, town squares (when used for events), Christmas markets, major civic festivals.

The responsible person for a local authority venue is typically the Chief Executive or a nominated senior officer. Non-compliance is a criminal liability.

## Integrated System as Compliance Framework

A documented Fyrfly lockdown procedure satisfies the communication and lockdown requirements for both tiers. Key technical provisions:

- **Single-trigger lockdown:** All controlled doors in venue secured in under 2 seconds
- **ARC notification:** Encrypted alert with live camera feed dispatched on trigger
- **Staff communication:** Push notification via secure network (not public mobile, which is frequently overwhelmed during major incidents)
- **Drill mode:** Full sequence executed without ARC or 999 notification; results logged for regulatory inspection
- **Tamper-evident audit log:** Every action timestamped; suitable for SIA inspectorate submission

## Temporary Events

Martyn's Law applies to events. A Christmas market in a council-managed town square that can hold 100+ people simultaneously triggers Standard Tier duties; larger events trigger Enhanced Tier.

Fyrfly rapid-deployment solar towers provide the monitoring and communications backbone for temporary events. Deployed in under 90 minutes; integrated with the council's permanent control room; ARC-connected throughout.
